# Scrum Approach

## 1. Why Scrum Was Used

Scrum was selected as the management framework for **AI Risk Detector** because the project involves uncertainty, product discovery and iterative improvement.

The assignment requires the use of Scrum, but Scrum is also appropriate for this project because the product idea can evolve through feedback. The team does not need to define a complete final solution from the beginning. Instead, the team can define a Product Backlog, select the most valuable items for the MVP, deliver an increment, collect feedback and adapt the backlog.

AI Risk Detector is also a good fit for Scrum because the product supports Agile software teams. The same concepts used to manage the project are also reflected in the product itself: backlog quality, Sprint Planning support, risk analysis, review, retrospective learning and continuous improvement.

Scrum helps the team organise the work through:

- clear roles;
- prioritised backlog items;
- sprint-based planning;
- visible progress;
- regular feedback;
- incremental delivery;
- retrospective improvement.

## 2. Scrum Roles

The Scrum roles are interpreted in the context of this academic project.

### Product Owner

The Product Owner is responsible for maximising the value of the product.

In this project, the Product Owner perspective is responsible for:

- defining the product vision;
- clarifying the problem to solve;
- prioritising the Product Backlog;
- deciding which user stories belong to the MVP;
- ensuring that user stories are aligned with the assignment objectives;
- validating whether the product delivers value for Agile and DevOps teams.

For AI Risk Detector, the Product Owner prioritises features that help teams identify risks earlier, improve backlog quality and support better planning or release decisions.

### Scrum Master

The Scrum Master is responsible for helping the team understand and apply Scrum effectively.

In this project, the Scrum Master perspective is responsible for:

- supporting Sprint Planning;
- helping the team keep the backlog understandable;
- promoting the use of Scrum events;
- identifying blockers or process problems;
- encouraging continuous improvement;
- ensuring that Sprint Review and Retrospective outcomes are documented.

For AI Risk Detector, the Scrum Master also helps connect the product with the Agile mindset: the tool should support collaboration, feedback and learning rather than create unnecessary bureaucracy.

### Developers

The Developers are responsible for creating the product increment.

In this project, the Developers are responsible for:

- structuring the repository;
- preparing the MVP concept;
- documenting product and technical decisions;
- supporting DevOps evidence;
- maintaining project documentation;
- ensuring that the increment is understandable and demonstrable.

The Developers also contribute to backlog refinement by identifying technical risks, dependencies and quality concerns.

## 3. Scrum Artifacts

Scrum artifacts help create transparency and support inspection and adaptation.

### Product Backlog

The Product Backlog is the ordered list of work needed for the product.

In this project, it is documented in:

- `docs/03_product_backlog.md`

The backlog includes user stories, priorities, acceptance criteria, MVP classification and Scrum/DevOps value.

It provides the basis for Jira issues and Sprint Planning.

### Sprint Backlog

The Sprint Backlog contains the Product Backlog items selected for a sprint, together with the plan for delivering them.

In this project, the Sprint Backlog is represented through:

- Jira sprint items;
- sprint planning documentation;
- sprint evidence files in the `management/` folder.

The Sprint Backlog helps the team focus on a clear Sprint Goal.

### Product Increment

The Product Increment is the usable output produced during a sprint.

In this project, increments may include:

- improved documentation;
- refined Product Backlog;
- MVP functionality or demonstration;
- Scrum evidence;
- DevOps documentation;
- responsible AI reflection;
- final report content.

Because this is an Agile Project Management assignment, the increment is not only software. Documentation, backlog quality, Scrum evidence and DevOps reasoning are also important parts of the project increment.

## 4. Scrum Events

Scrum events provide cadence, transparency, inspection and adaptation.

### Sprint Planning

Sprint Planning defines what will be worked on during the sprint and why it matters.

In this project, Sprint Planning is used to:

- define the Sprint Goal;
- select backlog items;
- clarify expected outputs;
- identify risks and assumptions;
- align the work with the MVP and assignment requirements.

Evidence is documented in:

- `management/sprint_1_planning.md`
- `management/sprint_2_planning.md`

### Daily Scrum

Daily Scrum is used to inspect progress and adapt the plan.

In this academic project, Daily Scrum can be represented through short team check-ins or informal updates focused on:

- what was completed;
- what is currently being worked on;
- blockers or dependencies;
- next actions.

The objective is not to create excessive documentation, but to maintain alignment and visibility during the project.

### Sprint Review

Sprint Review is used to inspect the increment and collect feedback.

In this project, Sprint Review is used to present:

- the current MVP or demo;
- Product Backlog progress;
- Scrum evidence;
- DevOps evidence;
- documentation improvements;
- feedback received from the professor, colleagues or team members.

Evidence is documented in:

- `management/sprint_1_review.md`
- `management/sprint_2_review.md`

### Sprint Retrospective

Sprint Retrospective is used to inspect how the team worked and define improvement actions.

In this project, Retrospective focuses on:

- what worked well;
- what could be improved;
- how collaboration can improve;
- how backlog quality can improve;
- how documentation and evidence can become clearer;
- how the next sprint can deliver more value.

Evidence is documented in:

- `management/sprint_1_retrospective.md`
- `management/sprint_2_retrospective.md`

## 5. Definition of Ready

A backlog item is considered **Ready** when it is clear enough to be selected for a sprint.

For this project, a backlog item is Ready when:

- the user story follows the format “As a..., I want..., so that...”;
- the expected value is understandable;
- acceptance criteria are defined;
- the priority is clear;
- the item is small enough to be addressed within a sprint;
- dependencies or assumptions are identified;
- the item is linked to the MVP or project objectives;
- the team understands what evidence is expected.

This Definition of Ready helps reduce ambiguity before Sprint Planning.

---

## 6. Definition of Done

A backlog item is considered **Done** when it satisfies the agreed quality and evidence expectations.

For this project, an item is Done when:

- the user story or task has been completed according to its acceptance criteria;
- the related documentation has been updated;
- the Jira status has been updated;
- relevant evidence has been added or referenced;
- the work is aligned with the Sprint Goal;
- the output supports the project deliverables;
- AI-generated content has been reviewed by humans when applicable;
- the item contributes to the MVP, Scrum evidence, DevOps evidence or final reflection.

This Definition of Done reinforces that completion is not only about producing content, but also about making the work visible, validated and connected to the project objectives.

Scrum is used not only to manage the project, but also as part of the product context.

AI Risk Detector supports Scrum teams by helping with:

- backlog refinement;

- acceptance criteria generation;

This creates a strong connection between the way the project is managed and the problem the product aims to solve.

The project applies Scrum while also building a product that supports Scrum activities.

## 8. Scrum Limitations and Learning

Scrum provides structure, cadence and feedback, but it does not automatically guarantee technical quality or delivery reliability.

For example, a team can follow Scrum events and still have weak testing, poor deployment practices or unclear release readiness. This is why DevOps is also important in this project.

Scrum helps the team decide what to work on, inspect progress and adapt the backlog. DevOps complements Scrum by focusing on quality, automation, delivery flow, testing, release readiness and feedback loops.

A key learning from this project is that Scrum and DevOps should not be treated as separate topics. Scrum provides the management framework, while DevOps strengthens the delivery and improvement capabilities of the team.

AI Risk Detector connects both areas by helping teams improve backlog clarity, identify risk earlier and support better decisions before execution or release.
