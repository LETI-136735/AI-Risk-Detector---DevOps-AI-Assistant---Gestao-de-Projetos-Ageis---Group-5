# Product Vision

## 1. Problem Statement

Agile software teams are expected to deliver value quickly, adapt to change and maintain a high level of quality. However, this becomes difficult when the team starts working with unclear, incomplete or poorly structured backlog items.

Common problems include:

- ambiguous requirements;
- user stories without clear acceptance criteria;
- technical tasks without enough risk analysis;
- missing test expectations;
- unclear dependencies;
- weak release preparation;
- limited visibility of delivery or quality risks.

When these problems are identified too late, they can create rework, sprint delays, failed deployments, production incidents or low confidence in the delivery process.

AI Risk Detector addresses this problem by helping teams analyse software tasks, requirements and backlog items before implementation or release.

The product supports earlier discussion about risk, quality and readiness, helping the team improve the work item before execution.

---

## 2. Target Users

AI Risk Detector is designed for Agile and DevOps software teams.

The main target users are:

### Product Owner

The Product Owner can use the assistant to improve backlog clarity, identify missing acceptance criteria and better understand the risk of prioritised items.

In this context, AI Risk Detector supports product value by helping the Product Owner make more informed backlog decisions.

### Scrum Master

The Scrum Master can use the assistant to support Sprint Planning, backlog refinement and retrospective discussions.

The tool can help identify unclear work items, possible blockers and improvement opportunities in the team workflow.

### Developers

Developers can use the assistant to understand possible technical risks, dependencies, testing needs and implementation concerns before starting work.

This supports better technical discussion and reduces the probability of late discovery of problems.

### DevOps Team Members

DevOps-oriented team members can use the assistant to identify delivery, deployment, rollback, monitoring or release readiness risks.

This is especially useful when the task involves production changes, infrastructure updates or release preparation.

### QA or Release Responsible

A QA-oriented team member or release responsible can use the assistant to review suggested tests, quality checks and acceptance criteria before approving a task or release.

---

## 3. Product Vision Statement

**AI Risk Detector helps Agile and DevOps software teams identify risks, improve backlog quality and support better planning, testing and release readiness decisions through AI-supported analysis of software tasks and requirements.**

The product is designed to support the team, not replace it.

AI recommendations must be interpreted as decision-support information. Final decisions remain the responsibility of the Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developers and other relevant stakeholders.

---

## 4. Value Proposition

AI Risk Detector creates value by supporting earlier and more structured discussion around software work items.

The main benefits are:

- earlier identification of delivery, quality and release risks;
- clearer backlog items;
- better acceptance criteria;
- stronger Sprint Planning discussions;
- improved release readiness;
- better alignment between Product Owner, Scrum Master and Developers;
- support for suggested tests and quality checks;
- reduced probability of rework;
- improved visibility of assumptions and dependencies;
- stronger support for continuous improvement.

The tool is especially useful when a task or requirement is written in a vague way and the team needs help identifying what is missing before execution.

---

## 5. In Scope

The product concept includes the following capabilities:

- submitting a software task, requirement or user story;
- analysing the item with AI-supported logic;
- classifying the risk level as Low, Medium or High;
- identifying potential risks;
- explaining possible impact;
- suggesting mitigation actions;
- suggesting tests or quality checks;
- proposing possible acceptance criteria;
- recommending improvements to the backlog item;
- supporting release readiness discussion;
- supporting Scrum events such as Sprint Planning, Review and Retrospective.

The MVP focuses on the most important part of this scope: allowing the user to enter a software task and receive a structured risk report.

---

## 6. Out of Scope

The project does not aim to deliver a complete enterprise-grade platform.

The following elements are outside the scope of the current MVP:

- full Jira API integration;
- real production monitoring;
- automated deployment control;
- enterprise-level AI governance;
- user authentication and role management;
- advanced analytics dashboards;
- historical risk trend analysis;
- real-time incident management;
- integration with cloud infrastructure tools;
- automatic approval or rejection of releases.

These elements may be considered as future improvements, but they are not required to demonstrate the academic objective of the project.

---

## 7. Success Criteria

The success of this project is evaluated through the coherence and quality of its Agile Project Management approach.

The project is considered successful if it demonstrates:

- a clear and realistic product vision;
- a well-structured and prioritised Product Backlog;
- user stories with meaningful acceptance criteria;
- clear Scrum roles, artifacts and events;
- evidence of Scrum management through Jira;
- a coherent DevOps interpretation;
- a functional or demonstrable MVP concept;
- responsible use of AI;
- critical reflection on the relationship between AI, Scrum and DevOps.

The value of the project is not measured only by the complexity of the application. It is also measured by how clearly the group applies Agile, Scrum and DevOps principles to a realistic product scenario.

---

## 8. Product Boundaries

AI Risk Detector should be understood as a **decision-support assistant**.

It can help generate suggestions, structure thinking and identify possible gaps. However, it cannot fully understand organisational context, business priorities, team capacity, technical architecture or production constraints without human input.

For this reason, every output generated by the assistant must be reviewed by the team.

The product boundary is clear:

> AI can support analysis, but humans remain accountable for decisions.

This principle is essential for the responsible use of AI in Agile and DevOps environments.
