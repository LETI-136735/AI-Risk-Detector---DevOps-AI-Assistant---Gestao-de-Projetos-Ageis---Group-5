# Sprint 1 Planning

## 1. Sprint Goal

The goal of Sprint 1 is to validate the core concept of **AI Risk Detector** by defining and demonstrating the first version of the MVP.

The sprint focuses on the basic user flow:

> A user enters a software task or requirement and receives a structured AI-supported risk analysis.

This sprint is not focused on building a complex application. The objective is to create a clear and demonstrable first increment that supports the academic goals of the project: Agile Project Management, Scrum application, DevOps reasoning and AI-supported decision-making.

---

## 2. Sprint Context

At the beginning of the project, the team identified that Agile and DevOps software teams may face problems such as unclear backlog items, ambiguous requirements, missing acceptance criteria and weak visibility of delivery risks.

Sprint 1 was planned to address the most important part of the product idea: helping the team identify risks before implementation or release.

The sprint also supports the creation of initial project evidence, including documentation, backlog structure and MVP definition.

---

## 3. Selected Backlog Items

The following backlog items were selected for Sprint 1.

| ID | User Story | Priority | Sprint Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| US01 | As a software team member, I want to enter a task, requirement or user story, so that I can submit it for risk analysis. | High | Required as the starting point of the MVP. |
| US02 | As a software team member, I want the assistant to analyse my input, so that I can understand possible risks before execution. | High | Core feature of the product. |
| US03 | As a Product Owner or Developer, I want to see a risk level, so that I can quickly understand the severity of the task. | High | Provides a simple and understandable result. |
| US04 | As a Developer, I want to see potential risks, so that I can understand what could go wrong before implementation. | High | Supports early risk identification. |
| US05 | As a Scrum team member, I want to understand the possible impact of a risk, so that the team can evaluate its importance. | High | Helps the team interpret the risk. |
| US06 | As a DevOps-oriented team member, I want mitigation recommendations, so that the team can reduce delivery or release risks. | High | Connects analysis with practical improvement actions. |

---

## 4. Rationale for Selection

The selected items represent the minimum functionality required to demonstrate the value of AI Risk Detector.

The team prioritised these stories because they support:

- the main product vision;
- early risk identification;
- Sprint Planning discussion;
- delivery and quality awareness;
- a simple but meaningful MVP;
- alignment with Agile and DevOps concepts.

The sprint does not include advanced features such as Jira integration, dashboards or historical analytics because these are not necessary for validating the first increment.

---

## 5. Expected Increment

At the end of Sprint 1, the team expects to have a first increment that demonstrates the following flow:

1. The user provides a software task or requirement.
2. The assistant analyses the input.
3. The assistant returns a risk level.
4. The assistant identifies potential risks.
5. The assistant explains the possible impact.
6. The assistant suggests mitigation actions.

The expected increment can be demonstrated through a functional MVP, mock-up, structured example or documented demo scenario.

---

## 6. Sprint Deliverables

The expected Sprint 1 deliverables are:

- initial MVP concept;
- initial Product Backlog structure;
- selected Sprint 1 backlog items;
- first risk analysis output example;
- initial documentation of Scrum approach;
- initial evidence structure in GitHub;
- Jira board setup or preparation.

---

## 7. Risks and Assumptions

| Risk or Assumption | Description | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| MVP scope may become too large | The team may try to build too many features too early. | Focus only on the minimum risk analysis flow. |
| AI output may be too generic | The generated analysis may not be specific enough. | Use realistic task examples and human review. |
| Documentation may become too long | The repository may become difficult to read. | Separate README from detailed documentation. |
| Jira and GitHub may become inconsistent | User stories may differ between tools. | Use the Product Backlog document as the reference. |
| App complexity may distract from the assignment | The project may become too technical. | Prioritise Agile, Scrum and DevOps evidence. |

---

## 8. Definition of Done for Sprint 1

Sprint 1 is considered complete when:

- the selected backlog items are documented;
- the MVP concept is clear;
- the first risk analysis flow is demonstrable;
- the Product Backlog is structured;
- Sprint 1 evidence is documented;
- Jira items are created or prepared;
- the repository explains the purpose of the project;
- the increment can be reviewed by the team or evaluator.
